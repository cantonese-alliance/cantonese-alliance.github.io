Proficiency Levels

Proficiency Guidelines

Cantonese Proficiency Standards

Beginning Level

Students build up a repertoire of vocabulary and basic grammatical structures so that they can:

  • introduce themselves in an informal social situation,
  • converse about themselves, their friends and families,
  • talk about activities in daily lives, and
  • engage in simple transactions.

Sample Curriculum

Samples of students' production:

  • Self Introduction-1st Quarter
  • Celebrities-1st Quarter
  • Buying fruit-1st Quarter
  • Fashion-2nd Quarter
  • Rapping about dim sum by beginning students
  • A presentation by a beginning student


Beginning Level for Mandarin Speakers and Learners

In addiiton to building up a repertoire of vocabulary and basic grammatical structures, Mandarin speakers will learn:

  • differences between basic Cantonese and Mandarin grammar and
  • differences between Cantonese and Mandarin pronunciation,

Samples of students' production:

  • Greetings and short conversations, 1st Quarter
  • Fruit and food, 1st Quarter
  • A Cantonese presentation by an advanced Mandarin learner
  • The City of Shenzhen by a native Mandarin speaker

Intermediate Level

The primary objective is to expand vocabulary and develop complex sentences. Learners acquire the vocabulary:

  • to engage in conversations about less concrete topics,
  • to give directions and instructions,
  • to carry out transactions in linguistically unfamiliar situations. and
  • to learn about major Chinese festivals and the legends associated with them

Samples of students' production:

  • The Legend of the Moon Goddess, 1st Quarter
  • Education, 1st Quarter
  • Spring Break-2nd Quarter

Intermediate-3 to Advanced-1 Level

The primary objective is to help students become proficient speakers so that they can talk about a wide range of topics:

  • history, e.g., the Cantonese language, SF Chinatown, the detention center on Angel Island, Tòihsāan, and Hong Kong
  • culture: movies and video clips, e.g., comparing how different regions welcome a new year
  • current events, e.g., COVID-19 and the general election in the U.S.
  • topics of interest to students, e.g., Bruce Lee and the theme of oppression in his movies, Asian American identity, language and identity, and Cantopop and identity

Students learn to describe and narrate in different time frames. Targeted linguistic skills include textual cohesion, subordinating and coordinating sentences, and linguistic devices for foregrounding and backgrounding information. There is also an optional field trip to a dim sum restaurant or a Cantonese community every quarter to experience Cantonese culture.

Samples of students' production:

  • The history of the Cantonese Language, 1st Quarter
  • Review of Mòuh Sēung, 1st Quarter
  • Review of "The Way We Are"
  • About Bruce Lee
