History of Hong Kong Cinema

  • Avenue of Stars: A Brief History of Hong Kong Movies.
  • Hidden Hong Kong: A history of Hong Kong cinema through the ages.
  • The Cinema of Hong Kong: History, Arts and Identity.
  • The Cinema of Hong Kong: History, Arts and Identity Hong Kong Cinema and Its Interaction with Global Culture. (This is a book review.)

Movies organized according to the decade(s) they appeared in:

  • 1970s:
    • Bruce Lee's kungfu movies: 《唐山大兄》 “The Big Boss” (1971),《猛龍過江 》"Way of the Dragon" (1972)
    • Hui Brothers' comedies: 《半斤八兩》 "The Private Eyes" (1976)
  • ​1980s-1990s:
    • Urban action movies: 《英雄本色》 (1986) "A Better Tomorrow"
    • Movies capturing the sentiments of and the arrivals of mainland Chinese in Hong Kong prior to its reversion to China in 1997: 《秋天的童話》 “An Autumn’s Tale”(1987),《胭脂扣》 "Rouge" (1987), 甜蜜蜜 “Comrade, Almost a Love Story” (1996)
    • New wave movies: Wong Kar-wai’s《阿飛正傳 》"Days of Being Wild" (1990) and 重慶森林 “Chungking Express” (1994)
  • 2000s:
    • Character-driven crime movies:《無間道 I, II, III》"Infernal Affairs I, II, III" (2002, 2003, 2003)
    • Stephen Chow’s most successful comedies:《少林足球》Shaolin Soccer” (2001),《功夫》Kung Fu Hustle (2004)
    • Wong Kar-wai’s most famous artsy movie: Wong Kar Wai’s 《花樣年華》 "In the Mood for Love" (2000)
  • 2010s - Now:
    • Movies based on true stories: 許鞍華 Ann Hui's《桃姐》"A Simple Life"  (2011), 《媽媽的神奇小子》 "Zero to Hero" (2021)
    • New directors: 陳小娟 Oliver Chan’s《淪落人》 "Still Human" (2018)
    • Parodies of older movies:《無雙》 “Project Gutenberg” (2018)
  • Popular Kungfu movies after Bruce Lee:
    • 《黃飛鴻》 “Once Upon a Time in China I, II, III, IV, V, VI” (1991, 1992, 1993, 1993, 1994, 1997): I like II.
    • 《方世玉 》 “Fong Sai Yuk” (1993)
    • 《葉問 》 “Ip Man I , II, III, IV” (2008, 2010, 2015, 2019): I like I and III.

Movie Samples

  • Zero to Hero (媽媽的神奇小子): This movie was released in August 2021 and is now available on Netflix. It's based on a true, inspiring story of a Hong Kong Paralympic sprinter.
  • A Better Tomorrow (英雄本色): This is a "must-watch" movie. It started a new genre of action drama known as heroic bloodshed in 1980s and had influenced Hollywood significantly.  However, it is how consistently the movie presents the Chinese traditional value of yih-hei (義氣) that most impresses me.  Here's the handout I've used for a 30-minute Cantonese practice session with intermediate to advanced learners. The handout talks about yih-hei (義氣) and heroic bloodshed.
  • Project Gutenberg (無雙):  Chow Yun-Fat acted in both A Better Tomorrow and Project Gutenberg. The latter can be perceived as a parody of the former.  His acting is always great and the story is suspenseful. The special effects are effective. Here's the handout I used in my advanced class and later modified to include more basic grammar and vocabulary.
  • Bruce Lee (李小龍): King of Kungfu Movies
    • "Enter the Dragon (龍爭虎鬥)" : Bruce Lee vs. Robert Wall
    • An interview with Casey about his experience with Bruce Lee when growing up in the U.S.
    • Casey's interview translated into Cantonese
  • "Shaolin Soccer (少林足球)": Funniest Kungfu parody by Stephen Chow (周星馳)
  • "Infernal Affairs (無間道)": Best Cop Movie from Hong Kong
  • Ann Hui (許鞍華): Best director of movies on social Issues, who directed the critically acclaimed movies "The Way We are" (天水圍的日與夜) in 2008 and "A Simple Life" (桃姐) in 2011.
  • "The Way We Are (天水圍的日與夜)": The life of residents in a public housing estate in Tin Shui Wai, Hong Kong
  • "A Simple Life" (桃姐)": The life of a woman who has worked for four generations of Producer Roger Lee's family.
  • "In the Mood for Love" (花樣年華): Best artsy movie directed by Wong Kar-Wai (王家衛); winner of Best Actor and Technical Grand Prize at the 2000 Cannes Film Festival.
  • I strongly recommend " Little Big Master (五個小孩的校長) to anyone who is interested in learning about social issues in Hong Kong, e.g., accessibility to preschool education, income disparty, and ethnic minorities.

Searchable movie database

  • Digital Museum of Cantonese: Created by Dr. Andy Chin of the Education University of Hong Kong. It is a searchable database of Cantonese movies from the mid 1950s.  When we can search a special expression (e.g. 新抱), entire sentences with the expression will appear.  This is a great tool for language learners.
