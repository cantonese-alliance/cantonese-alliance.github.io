Coordinator

Hello, I am Sik Lee Dennig or Jēung Sehk-leih (張錫莉) in Cantonese. I was born in Hong Kong to parents from Eastern China. I grew up in a multicultural environment in which Cantonese mingled with different varieties of the Wu language (吳語). I have taught middle schoolers in Hong Kong and college students in Canada, Japan, and the United States. I have coordinated the Cantonese program at Stanford University for over 20 years, growing it from one series to four. Language is tied intricately to culture, identity, history, and community, making language and linguistic courses fascinating to teach.

Graduate Education:
  • Dissertation: Cheung, Sik Lee. (1991). The Acquisition of Locative Constructions by Cantonese Children. Stanford University, CA, USA.
  • Master Thesis: Cheung, Sik Lee. (1986). Anaphora Production in Cantonese Narration. University of Alberta, Alberta, Canada.

  • Certified ACTFL Chinese Writing Proficiency Tester
